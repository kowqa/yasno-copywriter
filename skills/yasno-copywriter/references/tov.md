# Tone of Voice

## Core voice

Yasno explains psychology in simple language, but with subtlety and depth.

The shortest way to remember the tone:

- friendly, but not familiar
- smart, but not snobbish
- provocative, but not clickbait
- warm, but not clingy
- honest, but not harsh
- motivating, but without pressure

## Friendly, but not familiar

We speak with respect and equality. We do not flirt with the reader or imitate casual chat for the sake of closeness.

## Smart, but not snobbish

We like complex concepts, but explain them through situations, examples, and clear language.

Use:

- concrete examples
- plain language
- unpacked ideas

Avoid:

- jargon without explanation
- performative complexity
- academic heaviness

## Provocative, but not clickbait

We can ask uncomfortable questions. We can surprise. But we do not use cheap manipulation.

Bad:

- 10 признаков, что ваш партнер вас разрушает

Better:

- Всегда ли отношения должны приносить кайф? И если нет, значит ли это, что с ними что-то не так?

## Warm, but not clingy

We support the reader, but do not flatter them or over-praise routine actions.

## Honest, but not cruel

We do not avoid complexity. Therapy is difficult, life is difficult, people are complex. We tell the truth without brutality.

## Motivating, but without pressure

We can invite someone into movement, but not through panic, shame, or false certainty.

## Brand adjectives

Useful pairs from the internal materials:

- expert, but not textbook
- inviting, but not pushy
- human, but not servile
- partner-like, but not buddy-buddy
- complex, but not abstruse
- democratic, but not cheap
- progressive, but not cocky

## Copy test

If the text sounds like generic wellness marketing, it is wrong.

If it sounds like a dry lecture, it is wrong.

If it sounds like Yasno, it usually feels:

- clear
- grounded
- calm
- intelligent
- emotionally accurate

## How the skill works

This skill does not start from wording. It starts from context.

In practice, it should work like this:

1. understand the channel
2. understand the task
3. load Yasno core voice and restrictions
4. load the channel rules
5. load the task rules
6. look at relevant examples
7. choose an angle
8. write
9. self-check before returning

The point is not to generate random nice-sounding copy.

The point is to produce text that fits:

- Yasno voice
- the real channel
- the real goal of the text

## How an operator should work with the skill

The operator should not start with:

- `write a post`
- `make it beautiful`
- `make it selling`

The operator should start with a short brief:

- channel
- task
- topic
- audience
- desired action
- constraints

Good operator habit:

- first define the job of the text
- then ask for 2-3 angles
- then ask for the main draft
- then ask for revision if needed

The skill works best when the operator says:

- what channel this is for
- what kind of text this is
- what must happen after reading
- what must be avoided

## Operator checklist

Before accepting a draft, the operator should quickly ask:

- does this sound like Yasno?
- is the opening doing real work?
- is the text too generic?
- is there pressure, shame, or overpromising?
- does the text fit the channel mechanics?
- does the CTA match the meaning of the text?
