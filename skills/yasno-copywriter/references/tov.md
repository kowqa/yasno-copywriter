# Tone of Voice

## Core voice

Yasno explains psychology in simple language, but with subtlety and depth.

The shortest way to remember the tone:

- friendly, but not familiar
- smart, but not snobbish
- provocative, but not clickbait
- warm, but not clingy
- honest, but not harsh
- motivating, but without pressure

## Friendly, but not familiar

We speak with respect and equality. We do not flirt with the reader or imitate casual chat for the sake of closeness.

## Smart, but not snobbish

We like complex concepts, but explain them through situations, examples, and clear language.

Use:

- concrete examples
- plain language
- unpacked ideas

Avoid:

- jargon without explanation
- performative complexity
- academic heaviness

## Provocative, but not clickbait

We can ask uncomfortable questions. We can surprise. But we do not use cheap manipulation.

Bad:

- 10 признаков, что ваш партнер вас разрушает

Better:

- Всегда ли отношения должны приносить кайф? И если нет, значит ли это, что с ними что-то не так?

## Warm, but not clingy

We support the reader, but do not flatter them or over-praise routine actions.

## Honest, but not cruel

We do not avoid complexity. Therapy is difficult, life is difficult, people are complex. We tell the truth without brutality.

## Motivating, but without pressure

We can invite someone into movement, but not through panic, shame, or false certainty.

## Brand adjectives

Useful pairs from the internal materials:

- expert, but not textbook
- inviting, but not pushy
- human, but not servile
- partner-like, but not buddy-buddy
- complex, but not abstruse
- democratic, but not cheap
- progressive, but not cocky

## Copy test

If the text sounds like generic wellness marketing, it is wrong.

If it sounds like a dry lecture, it is wrong.

If it sounds like Yasno, it usually feels:

- clear
- grounded
- calm
- intelligent
- emotionally accurate
