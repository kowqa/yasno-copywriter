# Instagram

## Role of the channel

Instagram usually needs quick emotional readability, visual compatibility, and a strong opening.

In Yasno, Instagram is not just a distribution channel. It is a visual editorial format where the first slide, the carousel logic, and the caption each do different work.

## Priorities

- fast hook
- short paragraphs
- visual rhythm
- one clear idea
- high save/share value

## How Yasno tends to use Instagram

From the Instagram planning docs, the channel is built from several recurring content types:

- author carousels
- quote-based posts
- animated reels
- personal or experiential posts
- entertaining posts
- collaborations
- live announcements
- stories with native product transitions

The most important thing is that Instagram text must work on two levels at once:

1. inside the cards or the reel itself
2. inside the caption

## Card vs caption logic

This is one of the clearest rules in the Yasno Instagram docs.

### What should live in the cards

- the main idea
- the definition
- the strongest insight
- the conceptual progression
- anything the reader must understand without opening the caption

### What should live in the caption

- intrigue
- framing
- emotional entry
- a lighter bridge into the card sequence
- comment prompt or CTA when needed

The core rule:

- if the user only swipes the cards, the post must still work

## Slide logic

Instagram text in Yasno should be built slide by slide, not as a mini-article cut into pieces.

Typical carousel logic:

1. opener or title slide
2. definition, tension, or unexpected reframing
3. development of the thought
4. example, distinction, or implication
5. conclusion or reflective close

Some thoughts deserve their own slide. If a line carries an insight, let it breathe.

## Openers

Every carousel should have an opener on the first slide.

The Notion docs explicitly suggest offering 2-3 opener options.

Good opener types:

- a psychologically sharp question
- a line that names an inner contradiction
- a phrase that sounds simple but opens a deeper thought
- a myth or familiar belief that will be challenged

## Text density and air

Yasno's Instagram docs are clear about visual pacing:

- one card can hold up to around 800 characters, but that is the upper limit
- shorter is often better
- some cards should leave visual air for the designer
- not every card needs a heading

That means the writing should:

- compress without becoming cryptic
- leave space for images or visual emphasis
- avoid over-explaining on every slide

## Instagram format families in Yasno

### Family A. Insight carousel

This is the core format.

Typical structure:

1. strong opener
2. one concept or tension
3. 3-5 slides of development
4. reflective or re-framing close

### Family B. Question-to-self post

Seen in the `вопросы себе` bank.

These posts work through:

- one precise question
- emotional recognition
- reflection prompt

This format should feel probing, but not pseudo-deep.

### Family C. Short phrase or quote post

Seen in the `идеи коротких фраз` bank.

These posts work when the phrase:

- carries tension
- sounds memorable
- opens interpretation
- is not just aesthetic abstraction

Examples of the shape:

- `Меняться — это уметь терять`
- `Идеальное значит мертвое`
- `Жить сложнее, чем выживать`

### Family D. Adapted longform thought

Many Instagram ideas in Yasno come from:

- old essays
- Telegram columns
- blog texts

The goal is not to paste those texts into slides. The goal is to rebuild them into visual steps.

### Family E. Stories with native product bridge

The planning notes show that stories should sometimes lead natively into:

- therapist catalog
- tests
- product launches
- B2B

The important rule is that these transitions should feel editorially natural, not like a sudden ad break.

## What makes Yasno Instagram strong

- the first slide does real work
- the cards carry the main meaning
- the caption supports, but does not rescue the carousel
- abstract thoughts are grounded in psychological insight
- there is room for reflection, not just information

## What to avoid

- putting all the meaning into the caption
- overloading every card with dense text
- aesthetic emptiness disguised as wisdom
- generic pop-psychology slogans
- making the carousel read like a cut-up blog article
- writing cards with no visual rhythm in mind

## Good formats

- carousel headlines
- short body with a clear emotional insight
- compact educational breakdown
- comments prompt if needed
- question-to-self carousel
- short phrase post with interpretation
- adapted column or essay as visual steps
- native story bridge to product or test

## Avoid

- walls of text
- jargon-heavy explanations
- abstract intros
- fake intimacy
