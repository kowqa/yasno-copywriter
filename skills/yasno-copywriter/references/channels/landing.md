# Landing

## Role of the channel

Landing copy should reduce confusion, build trust, and move the person forward without hype.

## Priorities

- clear value proposition
- emotional accuracy
- proof points
- low-friction navigation
- consistency between headline, subhead, and CTA

## What Yasno landing copy tends to do

In the Notion landing drafts and live course pages, Yasno landing copy is usually:

- topic-first
- emotionally specific
- structurally clean
- low-pressure
- trust-building through explanation, not through inflated promises

The reader is usually invited into:

- a webinar
- a course
- a Telegram-based learning product

## Hero behavior

Yasno hero blocks usually combine three things:

1. A direct question or a sharply named problem.
2. A concise explanation of why this topic matters.
3. A clean action: register, go to the course, sign up in the bot.

Typical shape:

- `Как пережить расставание и не потерять себя?`
- `Как помогает терапия и когда она действительно нужна?`
- `Почему не получается построить отношения?`

What matters:

- the headline names the issue clearly
- the subhead deepens it with psychological specificity
- the CTA stays practical

## Repeating block logic in Yasno landings

### Block 1. What this is about

Hero:

- direct problem
- topic framing
- date / format
- CTA

### Block 2. Who this is for

Usually:

- `Если узнали себя...`
- a list of inner states, patterns, or situations

This works well because it lets the reader self-identify without shame.

### Block 3. What you will learn

Usually:

- `На вебинаре обсудим`
- `На курсе вы попробуете найти ответы на вопросы`

This section often works through:

- real questions
- distinctions between similar things
- common myths that will be unpacked

### Block 4. Program or speaker

If it is a course:

- detailed module list

If it is a webinar:

- concise agenda
- speaker authority

### Block 5. Format

Yasno is good at reducing friction here:

- where the course happens
- what the user gets after registration
- whether there is a recording
- whether there is a chat or bot

### Block 6. Why Yasno

This is usually the main proof block:

- first online psychotherapy service in Russia
- large number of psychologists
- manual selection and supervision
- number of people who have already used the service

### Block 7. FAQ

The FAQ usually does 3 jobs:

- remove friction
- clarify boundaries
- protect the therapeutic frame

One especially important move:

- speakers can answer questions about the topic
- they do not analyze a person’s private life on the webinar

That boundary is part of the brand.

## Language patterns that work especially well on Yasno landings

- questions instead of slogans
- `разберёмся`
- `обсудим`
- `поймём`
- `что происходит с психикой`
- `как может помочь психотерапия`
- `если узнали себя`

These verbs are useful because they promise orientation, not magic.

## Proof and CTA relationship

Yasno landings tend to work better when the proof appears after the explanation, not before it.

The reader first needs:

- recognition
- conceptual clarity
- emotional safety

Then:

- proof
- logistics
- CTA

## Thank-you pages

Even the thank-you pages keep the editorial tone.

Common moves:

- a quote
- a short acknowledgment
- a reminder that the person already took the first step
- a bot / email next-step explanation

## What to avoid

- generic market-speak
- loud claims about transformation
- fake scarcity as the main sales mechanism
- vague “best version of yourself” language
- pushing CTA before the reader understands the topic
- overstuffing the hero with too many promises

## Typical blocks

- hero
- explanation of the problem
- explanation of the service
- reasons to trust
- FAQ
- CTA

## Avoid

- inflated claims
- vague slogans
- inconsistent hierarchy
