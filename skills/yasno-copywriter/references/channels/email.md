# Email

## Role of the channel

Email is intimate and intentional. It should reward the open.

## Priorities

- honest subject line
- strong first screen
- one main action
- measured warmth
- clean CTA

## Patterns that recur in Yasno emails

Across the Notion examples, Yasno email usually works as:

1. A calm, recognizable opening:
   - `Привет, это Ясно!`
2. A concrete emotional or situational hook:
   - inner critic
   - first sessions
   - attachment styles
   - binge watching
   - pressure around gifts
3. A compact explanation of the phenomenon in clear language.
4. A small therapeutic reframe:
   - not just facts, but a new angle on the experience
5. A soft bridge to therapy, practice, or another useful action.
6. A single CTA, or at most two clearly separated CTAs.

The email should feel like:

- a useful note from a psychologically literate editor
- not a campaign blast
- not a sales letter in disguise

## Common subject line moves

Good Yasno subject lines tend to be:

- specific
- readable in one glance
- mildly intriguing
- emotionally accurate

Frequent patterns:

- a plain question
- a direct naming of the issue
- a contrast or opposition
- a small promise of clarity, not transformation

Examples of the shape:

- `Чего ждать от первых сессий?`
- `Избегающий, тревожный или надёжный?`
- `Не хочу покупать подарки: как быть?`
- `Почему мы смотрим сериалы запоем`

## Email body structure

### Structure A. Educational email

Use when explaining a concept:

1. Greeting
2. Why this topic matters
3. Short explanation of the phenomenon
4. 2-4 subpoints or examples
5. Bridge to therapy
6. CTA

### Structure B. Guide email

Use when reducing anxiety before action:

1. Greeting
2. Name the fear or uncertainty
3. Mini-guide with clear sections
4. What to expect / what not to expect
5. CTA

### Structure C. Series / partnership email

Use for a sequence or collaboration:

1. Greeting
2. Introduce the series and what it is for
3. Main psychological point
4. Insert from Yasno expert or psychologist
5. Partner block or practice block
6. Yasno therapy bridge
7. CTA
8. Teaser for the next email

## CTA behavior

Yasno emails often use CTA as a semantic extension of the text, not just a button label.

Examples of the tone:

- `Выбрать психолога`
- `Попробовать терапию`
- `Обсудить с тем, кто поймёт`
- `Начать сейчас`

The CTA should sound:

- calm
- clear
- consistent with the emotional logic of the email

Avoid:

- artificial urgency
- oversized promotional phrasing
- generic sales buttons detached from the body

## What works especially well in Yasno email

- naming a recognizable inner experience early
- explaining one concept well instead of covering too much
- short sections with subheads
- a practical question or reflection prompt
- a bridge from editorial value to therapy without tonal rupture

## What to avoid

- too many ideas in one email
- a long abstract intro
- a hard switch from educational tone to aggressive sales CTA
- overclaiming on therapy outcomes
- pseudo-scientific simplification
- sounding like a content farm or wellness funnel

## Default output

When writing email, include:

- 3 subject line options
- preview text
- main body
- CTA options

## Avoid

- spammy urgency
- all-caps energy
- fake scarcity
- overexplaining the obvious
