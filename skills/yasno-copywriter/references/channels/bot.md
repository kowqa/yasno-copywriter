# Bot

## Role of the channel

Bot text should be compact, predictable, and human without pretending to be a person.

In Yasno, bot copy is not just interface text. It is part of the service experience: warm enough to feel accompanied, clear enough to never create friction.

## Priorities

- short lines
- clear next step
- low ambiguity
- calm tone
- no unnecessary flourish

## What Yasno bot copy is trying to do

Across the webinar and course funnels in Notion, Yasno bot copy usually has one of these jobs:

1. register the person with minimal friction
2. deliver a promised bonus right away
3. remind about an event without sounding robotic
4. collect a small action:
   - name
   - email
   - phone
   - consent
   - a question to the speaker
5. continue the relationship after the event:
   - send recording
   - send extra materials
   - explain the next product
   - move into a soft promotional series

The copy should feel:

- operationally reliable
- emotionally accurate
- lightly warm
- never chaotic

## Structural patterns that recur

### Pattern A. Registration start

Use when the bot opens a webinar or course flow.

Typical structure:

1. greeting
2. date and time
3. what the event is about in one compact sentence
4. who leads it
5. promised bonus or value for registering
6. first micro-action

Example shape:

- `Привет!`
- `11 ноября в 19:00 по мск мы встретимся на вебинаре...`
- `Чтобы прислать ссылку и отправить гайд, нужно пройти регистрацию.`
- `Как вас зовут?`

### Pattern B. Field collection

Use when asking for name, email, phone, or consent.

Rules:

- ask for one thing at a time
- keep the instruction literal
- thank the person after each step
- do not add extra explanation unless legally required

Good shape:

- `Спасибо! Оставьте, пожалуйста, ваш email для регистрации.`
- `Для завершения регистрации, подтвердите согласие на обработку данных.`

### Pattern C. Bonus delivery

Use when the person gets something immediately after registration.

Rules:

- remind them why this bonus matters
- keep the promise concrete
- deliver fast
- do not overhype the material

Good shape:

- `Как и обещали, отправляем вам инструкцию...`
- `Дарим полезный гайд...`

### Pattern D. Reminder before event

Use on the day before, the morning of, or one hour before a webinar.

Rules:

- remind the person what will happen
- use one short list if needed
- repeat the time clearly
- reduce friction to joining

Good shape:

- `Вебинар уже завтра`
- `Сегодня в 19:00`
- `Через час`
- `Подключиться`

### Pattern E. After-event promo

Use after a webinar, live event, or mini-course when the next offer is therapy or a paid course.

Rules:

- start with thanks
- briefly summarize the value of the event
- offer the next step as support, not pressure
- keep legal promo language intact where required

Common flow:

1. thank you
2. one sentence on what was discussed
3. next offer
4. promo code / timing if relevant
5. one clean CTA

### Pattern F. Nurture after event

Use in the days after a webinar or bonus delivery.

This is where Yasno often sends:

- a test
- a reflection prompt
- an article
- a myth-busting note
- a short explanation of therapy in this situation

The key is to keep each message centered on one idea only.

## Tone rules specific to bot copy

Compared to email or Telegram, bot copy should be:

- shorter
- more literal
- less essayistic
- more action-oriented

Warmth should show up in:

- polite phrasing
- light reassurance
- clean gratitude
- a calm sense of accompaniment

Warmth should not show up as:

- jokes that slow the action
- long emotional preambles
- poetic language
- too much brand voice packed into one message

## Reusable Yasno moves in bot copy

- start from a concrete moment: `сегодня`, `завтра`, `через час`
- name the topic in emotionally precise language
- validate without over-dramatizing
- offer one next step only
- keep buttons simple and explicit
- use therapy as support, not as rescue fantasy

## What works especially well in Yasno bot funnels

- immediate bonus after registration
- reminders that add a little useful context instead of repeating the same line
- collecting questions for the speaker before the event
- post-event sequences that keep helping before selling
- careful bridges from editorial value to therapy or courses

## What to avoid

- two actions in one message
- long blocks before a required input
- vague button labels
- trying to sound too witty or too intimate
- loading legal or promo copy with brand flourishes
- abrupt switching from supportive tone to aggressive offer

## Avoid

- long explanations
- over-familiarity
- vague buttons or choices
