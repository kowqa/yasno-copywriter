# Ethics And Restrictions

This file exists to prevent avoidable damage.

## General rule

If a phrase is likely to make a person feel shamed, cornered, mocked, or exposed, rewrite it.

## Sensitive wording

Avoid loaded or stigmatizing labels.

Examples from the internal materials:

- `домохозяйка`
- `мать-одиночка`
- `женщина легкого поведения`

## Mothers and guilt

Be especially careful with any text about mothers. Do not intensify guilt. Do not make the mother figure into a convenient villain.

## Heavy psychoanalytic content

Avoid dense, old-fashioned psychoanalytic formulations unless they are necessary and carefully explained.

If a difficult term must stay, preface it and translate it into normal language.

## Legal and policy sensitivity

Check text against current legal and platform constraints before publishing on sensitive topics.

The existing internal note explicitly flags some topics as high-risk. Treat those topics with extra caution and escalate for human review when needed.

## "Traditional values" sensitivity

Some complex psychoanalytic ideas can sound inflammatory or ideological when compressed into marketing copy. If a concept requires several paragraphs to justify, it probably should not appear in a short public text.

## Final ethical test

Before shipping, ask:

- Could this phrase provoke shame?
- Could this phrase sound like a diagnosis?
- Could this phrase promise too much?
- Could this phrase be interpreted as pressure?
- Could this phrase trigger a public backlash because it oversimplifies a sensitive issue?

If yes, rewrite.
