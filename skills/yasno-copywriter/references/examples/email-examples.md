# Email Examples

This file captures useful patterns from Yasno email drafts in Notion.

It is not a swipe file to copy blindly. Use it to understand structure, tone, and editorial moves.

## Example 1. Mini-guide before therapy

Source:

- `чего ждать от сессий`

Task:

- educational
- promo

Why it works:

- opens from a real user fear
- reduces uncertainty before the first session
- splits the content into `what to expect` and `what not to expect`
- moves to therapy naturally, because the whole email is already about starting therapy

Reusable patterns:

- `Мы знаем, что перед первой сессией многие чувствуют тревогу`
- `Этот мини-гайд поможет почувствовать себя увереннее`
- section contrast: expectation vs misconception
- CTA tied to the exact action the email prepares for

Do not copy blindly:

- the exact session numbers or structure if the user request is about a different product

## Example 2. Educational email with built-in test

Source:

- `теория привязанности`

Task:

- educational
- engagement
- promo

Why it works:

- starts from a popular concept that people already recognize
- gives a compact conceptual explanation
- moves quickly to typology, which keeps attention
- includes a test as a low-friction interaction
- ends with therapy as a space to work deeper, not as a magic fix

Reusable patterns:

- explain a concept through origin story
- introduce 2-3 recognizable categories
- insert a low-commitment action in the middle
- end with a careful statement that therapy can help explore the deeper layer

## Example 3. Seasonal emotional question

Source:

- `К черту подарки`

Task:

- educational
- support
- promo

Why it works:

- starts from a socially awkward but familiar feeling
- uses a reader question as the frame
- validates the feeling before suggesting any action
- gives practical steps without sounding prescriptive
- therapy is offered as a continuation, not a correction

Reusable patterns:

- quote a reader dilemma
- answer through a named expert
- structure the body as `what to do with this feeling`
- end with a gentle therapy bridge

## Example 4. Reflective monthly word

Source:

- `СЛОВО МАРТА доверяю`

Task:

- engagement
- educational

Why it works:

- treats the email as an editorial ritual, not just a campaign
- creates continuity month to month
- introduces one word and unfolds it psychologically
- includes questions for self-reflection
- ends with therapy as a place to continue the inquiry

Reusable patterns:

- monthly or recurring rubric
- one-word frame
- `О чём оно для нас?`
- `О чём оно для вас?`
- reflective question block

## Example 5. Topic-driven explainer

Source:

- `binge watching`

Task:

- educational
- promo

Why it works:

- starts from a highly recognizable everyday behavior
- names the phenomenon clearly
- avoids immediate moral judgment
- explains the mechanism in accessible language
- includes reflection questions before the CTA

Reusable patterns:

- `Это что, плохо? В целом, нет`
- explain the mechanism without over-academic tone
- add 2-3 self-reflection questions
- bridge to therapy as help with the underlying pattern, not with the symptom only

## Example 6. Series email with partner integration

Source:

- `Просто (или не просто?)`

Task:

- announcement
- educational
- partnership
- sales

Why it works:

- gives the series a clear container
- each letter has one tight psychological angle
- Yasno and partner roles are separated cleanly
- the therapy CTA and partner CTA do not collapse into one confusing ask

Reusable patterns:

- announce the mini-series at the top
- one letter, one psychological step
- insert `мысль от психолога`
- insert `практика от партнёра`
- tease the next letter at the end

Warning:

- partnership emails need especially careful CTA hierarchy
- if there are two offers, they must be clearly separated in structure and intent

## Cross-example conclusions

What Yasno email tends to do well:

- one letter, one real idea
- one emotional tension per text
- useful framing before conversion
- therapy as a natural continuation of reflection
- CTA language that matches the meaning of the letter

What to preserve when writing new email copy:

- calm authority
- editorial usefulness
- emotional precision
- soft but clear conversion logic
