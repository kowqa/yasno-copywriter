# Longread Examples

These examples are drawn from Yasno blog articles and long-form explainers.

Use them to understand how Yasno builds depth, keeps complex topics readable, and moves from orientation to support without turning the article into a sales page.

## Example 1. Clarifying professional roles

Source:

- `В чем разница между психологом, психиатром и психотерапевтом?`

Task:

- educational
- myth-busting
- service explanation

Why it works:

- starts from a question readers genuinely have
- admits that the confusion is understandable
- separates the terms one by one
- uses a concrete example to show the practical difference
- ends with a useful next step rather than abstract theory

Reusable patterns:

- begin with `этот вопрос нам задают чаще всего`
- explain why the topic is confusing before defining terms
- dedicate one section to each term
- insert one real-life example to make the distinction tangible

## Example 2. Fear-reducing symptom explainer

Source:

- `Паническая атака: симптомы, причины и что делать во время приступа`

Task:

- educational
- support
- service bridge

Why it works:

- names the experience directly and without drama
- combines symptom list, mechanism, and practical steps
- uses headings that make the article easy to navigate
- reduces fear without dismissing the severity of the experience
- explains when self-help is enough and when professional support matters

Reusable patterns:

- define the state in the first paragraph
- move from `what it is` to `what to do`
- include step-by-step support actions
- add sections like `как отличить`, `когда обратиться`, `FAQ`

## Example 3. Reframing a morally loaded emotion

Source:

- `Как устроена агрессия (и почему не стыдно её проявлять)?`

Task:

- educational
- emotional reframing

Why it works:

- starts from a broad truth about emotions
- reframes aggression as a signal and function, not just a problem
- names both suppressed and distorted forms of aggression
- keeps a clear moral stance without becoming preachy

Reusable patterns:

- open with a larger psychological frame
- challenge the common shame around the topic
- explain the healthy function of the feeling
- show what happens when the feeling is denied or distorted

## Example 4. Topic explainer with practical orientation

Sources:

- `Как понять, что мне нужен психолог?`
- `Как выбрать психотерапевта`

Task:

- educational
- support
- service explanation

Why they work:

- they answer practical questions readers are often embarrassed to ask
- they combine reassurance with useful criteria and distinctions
- they reduce ambiguity around starting therapy
- they help the reader move from abstract interest to a more grounded choice

Reusable patterns:

- name the practical decision early
- list concrete signs, questions, or criteria
- explain what the reader can look for in themselves or in a professional
- make the CTA a continuation of clarity, not a push

## Example 5. Dense concept in accessible language

Sources:

- `Уровни организации личности`
- `Что такое созависимость?`
- `Расстройства пищевого поведения`

Task:

- educational
- concept explanation

Why they work:

- they take topics that could sound academic and make them readable
- they introduce theory gradually instead of dumping terminology up front
- they keep translating the concept into lived experience

Reusable patterns:

- define a dense concept in one plain sentence
- then expand it through manifestations and examples
- avoid jargon unless it is immediately unpacked
- make the practical meaning of the concept visible

## Example 6. Longread as orientation plus next step

Source pool:

- blog articles with tests, therapist selection links, or support blocks

Task:

- educational
- conversion support

Why it works:

- the article already gives value before any CTA appears
- the next step is tied to the topic:
  - test
  - choosing a therapist
  - understanding whether help is needed
- conversion feels like guidance, not interruption

Reusable patterns:

- put the support step after enough explanation
- tie the CTA to the exact reader question raised by the article
- keep the invitation calm and specific

## Cross-example conclusions

What Yasno longreads tend to do well:

- make psychologically complex topics readable
- respect the reader's intelligence
- explain without flattening
- structure long texts so they are easy to scan
- connect theory, experience, and practical orientation

What to preserve in new longreads:

- one clear thesis
- visible section logic
- examples that ground the theory
- calm expert voice
- useful conclusion that may lead to support, but does not depend on the sale
