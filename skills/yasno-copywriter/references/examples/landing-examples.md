# Landing Examples

These examples come from Yasno course and webinar landing drafts in Notion.

The goal is not to mirror the exact page structure every time, but to understand what Yasno landing copy is doing well.

## Example 1. Relationship course landing

Source:

- `лендинг` in the `отношения` project

Task:

- landing
- sales
- educational

Why it works:

- the hero names the pain clearly: relationships do not just “work out”
- the subhead immediately reframes the issue inward: expectations, fears, limitations
- the `для кого` block uses self-recognition rather than aspirational fluff
- the program is framed as reflection and understanding, not tips and hacks

Reusable patterns:

- `Если узнали себя, этот курс для вас`
- use questions as curriculum headlines
- explicitly say what the product is not, for example `это не сборник практических советов`

## Example 2. Emotional regulation webinar

Source:

- `Лендинг “как управлять эмоциями”`

Task:

- landing
- webinar registration

Why it works:

- the hero names 3 adjacent questions, which widens relevance without losing focus
- the promise is practical, but not simplistic
- the `для кого` block uses states the reader can easily recognize in themselves
- the `почему Ясно` section explains the philosophical stance, not just company facts

Reusable patterns:

- cluster 2-3 related questions in the hero
- explain what stands behind visible symptoms
- use the `почему Ясно` block to state the educational philosophy

## Example 3. Depression webinar

Source:

- `лендинг депрессия`

Task:

- landing
- educational
- webinar registration

Why it works:

- the topic is sensitive, but the copy stays calm and specific
- it introduces two frames: medical and psychoanalytic
- the `для кого` block respects the reader’s state and avoids melodrama
- the agenda is concrete and appropriately bounded

Reusable patterns:

- contrast two explanatory frameworks without false certainty
- use symptom language carefully
- make the CTA simple and stable

## Example 4. Breakup webinar

Source:

- `лендинг расставание`

Task:

- landing
- webinar registration

Why it works:

- the hero combines pain and identity loss, which makes the topic deeper than “how to move on”
- the `для кого` list captures both fresh and old pain
- the content agenda includes invalidating advice the audience already knows does not work

Reusable patterns:

- name both the event and its psychic consequences
- include lines like `вроде бы всё понимаете, но вам всё равно больно`
- show that the webinar will explain why simple advice fails

## Example 5. Psychotherapy explainer webinar

Source:

- `лендинг психотерапия внутри ясно`

Task:

- landing
- educational
- trust-building

Why it works:

- it starts from a common fantasy about therapy: someone will explain and fix everything
- it promises orientation instead of salvation
- it includes practical decision questions: when therapy is needed, how to choose, what changes to expect
- it directly addresses the “can AI replace a therapist?” style myth without sounding defensive

Reusable patterns:

- begin from a cultural misunderstanding
- move into realistic explanation
- use the landing to lower the threshold to starting therapy

## Cross-example conclusions

What Yasno landing copy consistently does well:

- names a real difficulty in plain words
- explains why the topic matters psychologically
- uses `для кого` as a mirror, not as segmentation jargon
- promises clarity, understanding, and reflection rather than transformation
- places trust signals after the person already feels understood
- uses FAQ to remove friction and protect boundaries

What to preserve in future landing copy:

- direct question-based headlines
- calm explanatory subheads
- recognition-driven audience blocks
- one coherent educational promise
- practical CTA with minimal noise
