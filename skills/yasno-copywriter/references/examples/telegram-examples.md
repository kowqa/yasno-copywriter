# Telegram Examples

These examples are drawn from Yasno Telegram-style posts and post-like long captions from the Notion corpus.

Use them to understand how Yasno thinks on the page.

## Example 1. A familiar comparison that reveals the value of therapy

Source:

- `Внимание терапевта vs дружеское`

Task:

- educational
- promo

Why it works:

- starts from a very common objection: `зачем терапевт, если есть друг`
- does not mock the objection
- carefully distinguishes therapy from friendship through specific qualities
- builds trust in therapy without cheap selling

Reusable patterns:

- contrast two kinds of support
- break the contrast into numbered qualities
- keep the tone explanatory rather than defensive

## Example 2. Popular belief -> deeper correction

Source:

- `4 мифа о методах терапии`

Task:

- educational
- myth-busting

Why it works:

- starts from a claim people have heard before
- complicates it instead of replacing one simplification with another
- uses evidence and names researchers without becoming academic sludge
- ends with a relational truth that feels bigger than the initial myth

Reusable patterns:

- `Миф 1`, `Миф 2` framing
- one myth per section
- use research selectively to support the main point
- move from technical misunderstanding to a more human conclusion

## Example 3. Relationship/system thinking

Source:

- `Когда и зачем нужна парная терапия?`

Task:

- educational
- service explanation

Why it works:

- translates a therapy format into everyday language
- explains the shift from “one bad partner” to “the system”
- uses examples of recognizable relationship problems
- avoids promising that therapy will fix the couple quickly

Reusable patterns:

- introduce the systemic frame early
- use direct examples of situations readers recognize
- explain the process of therapy, not just the benefits

## Example 4. Ambivalence as the center of the post

Source:

- `Хочу бросить терапию: что за этим стоит`

Task:

- educational
- emotional reframing

Why it works:

- starts from an experience that might feel taboo or shameful
- normalizes the urge without flattening it
- maps several deeper explanations
- turns doubt itself into material for reflection

Reusable patterns:

- start from a difficult impulse
- split it into hidden drivers
- show that the impulse may contain meaning
- end with a more nuanced understanding, not a command

## Example 5. Big transformation topic with psychological dignity

Source:

- `Эмиграция как путь героя`

Task:

- longform Telegram
- educational
- existential

Why it works:

- frames migration as both external event and inner transformation
- uses a cultural/mythic frame without sounding pompous
- respects grief, disillusionment, and adaptation
- makes the text feel larger than practical advice

Reusable patterns:

- combine cultural frame with psychological mechanism
- move through stages
- acknowledge loss before adaptation
- end on integration, not on optimism theater

## Example 6. Provocative topic, careful treatment

Source:

- `Почему люди ходят к гадалкам`

Task:

- educational
- provocative

Why it works:

- starts from a topic easy to ridicule
- refuses ridicule
- explains the psychological need underneath magical thinking
- keeps a respectful distance from both mockery and endorsement

Reusable patterns:

- take a culturally loaded topic
- translate it into psychological language
- explain the need beneath the behavior

## Example 7. Dense concept, simple language

Source:

- `Алекситимия`

Task:

- educational

Why it works:

- names a term, then immediately unpacks it
- gives multiple manifestations of the same condition
- never hides behind terminology
- shows why the concept matters in life, not only in theory

Reusable patterns:

- define a term
- show how it can look in practice
- give a developmental explanation
- end with the therapeutic significance

## Example 8. Book/content post with real point of view

Sources:

- `Тайная опора`
- `Синдром предков`

Task:

- educational
- recommendation

Why they work:

- they do not just say “good book”
- they extract the live psychological value of the text
- they use quotations and framing to make the recommendation meaningful

Reusable patterns:

- use a recommendation as a way to teach, not just promote
- choose a strong interpretive angle
- make the book relevant to the reader’s life

## Cross-example conclusions

What Yasno Telegram tends to do well:

- names something recognizable
- opens the hidden layer beneath it
- gives the reader a more interesting explanation than they expected
- stays calm even on loaded topics
- treats psychological complexity as normal

What to preserve in new Telegram writing:

- thought density
- conceptual clarity
- emotional tact
- confidence without posturing
- a sense that the text slightly changes how the reader sees the issue
