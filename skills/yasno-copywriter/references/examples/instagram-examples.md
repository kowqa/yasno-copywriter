# Instagram Examples

These examples are drawn from Yasno Instagram planning pages, format banks, and references to published posts.

Use them to understand how Yasno adapts psychological thinking to a visual, swipe-based format.

## Example 1. Insight-first carousel

Sources:

- Instagram planning page technical rules
- references like `Мифы поп-психологии`
- `Обратная сторона эмпатии`

Task:

- educational
- saveable carousel

Why it works:

- the first slide makes a clear promise
- the main meaning lives in the cards, not in the caption
- the idea is split into visually manageable steps
- the sequence teaches one concept well instead of trying to cover everything

Reusable patterns:

- offer 2-3 opener options
- begin the cards from the definition or the sharpest point
- let one important insight take a slide of its own
- use the caption as framing, not as the actual article

## Example 2. Question-to-self format

Source:

- `вопросы себе` bank in `insta’2026`

Task:

- engagement
- reflective content

Why it works:

- the questions are psychologically sharp, not generic
- they invite self-observation without sounding like coaching prompts
- they feel intimate, but still brand-consistent

Reusable patterns:

- ask one emotionally precise question
- make the question concrete enough to trigger recognition
- avoid “best self” or self-help clichés

Example shapes:

- `Я на своей стороне?`
- `Что я выдаю за силу, хотя это защита?`
- `Чего я избегаю, называя это спокойствием?`

## Example 3. Short phrase with depth

Source:

- `идеи коротких фраз` bank in `insta’2026`

Task:

- quote-style post
- reflective carousel

Why it works:

- the line is brief, but not empty
- it carries psychological tension
- it can open into a larger thought in the caption or following slides

Reusable patterns:

- use a short line that contains contradiction or density
- make sure the phrase invites unpacking
- avoid decorative vagueness

Examples of the shape:

- `Меняться — это уметь терять`
- `Бездействие — новое действие`
- `Обида — это способ сохранить контакт`

## Example 4. Adapting longform into carousel structure

Sources:

- `старые тексты, которые подойдут`
- linked topics such as:
  - `повтор сценариев`
  - `Нужно ли прощать родителей`
  - `Не всякая фрустрация — это травма`

Task:

- repurposing
- educational carousel

Why it works:

- Yasno already has strong longform material
- Instagram becomes effective when that material is rebuilt into steps, not pasted whole
- the best adapted posts preserve the thesis while simplifying the visual rhythm

Reusable patterns:

- extract one thesis from the original text
- split it into 4-6 swipeable units
- keep each card legible on its own
- move extra framing into the caption

## Example 6. Format planning as part of brand voice

Sources:

- `объем публикаций в месяц`
- `гипотезы инста`
- `идеи форматов`

Task:

- channel design
- editorial planning

Why it works:

- Yasno treats Instagram as a mix of depth, play, and product
- the channel is not only expert education
- it also needs rhythm:
  - author texts
  - reels
  - entertainment
  - collaborations
  - live sessions
  - stories

Reusable patterns:

- do not make every Instagram output look like a serious essay
- preserve different tonal zones while keeping the same brand core
- vary between educational, playful, reflective, and promotional formats

## Cross-example conclusions

What Yasno Instagram tends to do well:

- separates the logic of cards and caption
- relies on strong first-slide hooks
- gives psychologically meaningful content a visual structure
- allows reflective depth without becoming too wordy
- uses short phrases and questions as real editorial devices, not decoration

What to preserve in new Instagram writing:

- opener discipline
- slide-by-slide thinking
- visual air
- one main idea per carousel
- emotionally precise but non-generic reflective language
